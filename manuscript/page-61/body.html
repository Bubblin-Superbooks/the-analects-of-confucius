Xxiii.

The Master said, 'A cornered vessel without corners.— A strange cornered vessel! A strange cornered vessel!'

Xxiv.

Tsai Wo asked, saying, 'A benevolent man, though it be told him,— 'There is a man in the well' will go in after him, I suppose.' Confucius said, 'Why should he do so?' A superior

man may be made to go to the well, but he cannot be made to go down into it. He may be imposed upon, but he cannot be fooled.'

Xxv.

The Master said, 'The superior man, extensively studying all learning, and keeping himself under the restraint of the rules of propriety, may thus likewise not overstep what is right.'
