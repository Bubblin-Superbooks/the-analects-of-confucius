Tsze-lu, Tsang Hsi, Zan Yu, and Kung-hsi Hwa were sitting by the Master. He said to them, 'Though I am a day or so older than you, do not think of that.

'From day to day you are saying, "We are not known." If some ruler were to know you, what would you like to do?' Tsze-lu hastily and lightly replied, 'Suppose the case of a State of ten thousand chariots; let it be straitened between other large States; let it be suffering from invading armies; and to this let there be added a famine in corn and in all vegetables:— if I were intrusted with the government of it, in three years' time I could make the people to be bold, and to recognise the rules of righteous conduct.' The Master smiled at him. Turning to Yen Yu, he said, 'Ch'iu, what are your wishes?'
