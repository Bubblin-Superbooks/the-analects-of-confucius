Xviii.

The Duke of Sheh asked Tsze-lu about Confucius, and Tsze-lu did not answer him. The Master said, 'Why did you not say to him,— He is simply a man, who in his eager pursuit (of knowledge forgets his)

food, who in the joy of its attainment forgets his sorrows, and who does not perceive that old age is coming on?'

Xix.

The Master said, 'I am not one who was born in the possession of knowledge; I am one who is fond of antiquity, and earnest in seeking it there.'
